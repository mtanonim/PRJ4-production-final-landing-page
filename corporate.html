Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

  • A colour scheme (with HTML colour codes, which must be consistent with your final website):
    • #0093F5 This light blue was used for navbar and footer to tie the whole page together, establishing it's borders in gentle way. This blue stands for sophistication and reliability.
    • #00406B This dark blue was used mainly for fonts - it is dark to create some contrast with the background and "highlight" information. This blue stands for corporate and high quality
    • #FEF8A6 This yellow was used for the background of the website, making it more saturated, evoking more positive thoughts. This yellow stands for happiness.
    • #FF91F9 This hot pink was used for fonts - again trying to create some contast and make headers pop. This pink stands for sincerity and is flirty.
  • Font choices

  • lust-serif -> used for main headers and navbar. We used a serif font to make it more playfull fitting our brand, most of the time complimenting contrast colors.

  • lust-dione-fine -> used for the brand name, differentiating a bit from other text. Close to previous font, but san serif, making it fell more professional and sophisticated, while still being on brand with the playful fell.

  • Raleway -> used for normal text and announcment, as it is easily readable, also thanks to the fact of being san serif. Used for plain text

  • User interface patterns (e.g. grids, carousels, menu organizations etc.)
  • Navigation bar -> used for a coherant navigation on top of the page. Also includes a dropdown menu for more options, while maitaining its simplicity.
  • Carousel -> used an owlcarousel which adds more features like auto scroll, hover pause and dynamic number of items. Used for rating and team presentation to make it more dynamic and engaging
  • Links -> used link internal on the page linking to other parts of the code/pages of the website and externaly to our social media, making eeverything more accesible.
  • Footer -> used on the bottom the page for some navigation to our social media, top of the page and copyright
  • Video container/iframe -> used on our landing/idex page to grab immediate attention, simply showing what our service is about. It has a transparent div overlaying it making it impossible to click.
  • Divs -> using divs which utilize flexbox to structure the page, making it responsive. Also overlaying the video as mentioned before.
  • Buttons -> using buttons which uses link to direct to content (mostly ouside of the page)
  • The structure of the navigation and content (e.g. how content units are distributed across pages)

  • The whole page is structured with repetetive navbars and footers to make the controls same and not confusing. The navbar has 4 main part, making it not overwhealming, when looking for something quickly. Other parts of the of the body use flexbox to be responsive. Each part has some sort of visual design = picture, video or carousel with pictures to grab the attention of the user. This is complimented by contrasting colors in headers. Links provide aditional navigation through the site, while also providing redirection to external content which is relevant to the brand/product.

Lean Canvas version 3

Fill in at least 2 bullet points per building block and according to the rubrics.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group(s):
  • Loneliness
  • Normal dates/dating apps feel forced
  • People on dating apps often have different intentions
  • Right now, these problems are mostly already separately solved by ‚Äònormal‚Äô (like tinder) dating apps and activity sign ups.
  • These problems were all uncovered in our problem interviews. Our interviewees said that they tried to keep themselves busy so that they would not feel so lonely. And that it was difficult to find someone at their age who had the same intentions as they did, since many people already have children or still really want to have children for example.

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution(s) you have for the above problem(s).
  • Sign up for group activities → do something fun together to solve the loneliness
  • Sign up for the activity instead of a specific person → does not make it feel forced
  • You get a badge on your profile and during the activity stating your intentions → per activity it states how much percent of people with a specific badge have signed up already

Customer segments

  • List your target customers and/or users.
  • Describe briefly the characteristics of your ideal customer (early adopter, brand persona).
  • Middle aged singles
  • Have a fulltime job that they devote their lives to
  • Feel alone (do not have to be all the time)
  • Like to keep themselves busy (hobbies)
  • Want to meet people in a spontaneous way (it feels better and more genuine)
  • Love their family

Unique value proposition

  • With a single, clear compelling message, state why your brand idea is different and worth paying attention to.

  • Our brand and product idea are different from other dating apps because people are able to sign up for the group activity instead of a specific person. You will meet people along the way while participating in a fun activity. This way it does not feel forced, but you will still meet people that have the same intentions as you do.
